About

Short professional biography (editable).

Bio

I am a tourism lecturer and researcher with interests in Muslim-friendly tourism, inclusion and representation, and the social dynamics of travel in predominantly non-Muslim contexts. My work examines how mobility, place, and everyday encounters shape perceptions of belonging and difference.

I also contribute to tourism education through curriculum design, tutorial teaching, and student support.

Academic interests

  • Muslim-friendly tourism: beyond halal compliance to social inclusion
  • Mobilities & transnationalism in everyday travel practices
  • Sustainable tourism, ethics, and destination governance
  • Digital tourism: representation, aesthetics, and decision-making

Research

Core themes and what you are currently working on.

Theme 1: Inclusion as lived experience

How Muslim tourists interpret safety, welcome, and visibility in tourism settings, and how inclusion changes over repeated visits.

Theme 2: Tourism spaces & power

How destination infrastructures, narratives, and institutional norms shape boundaries and encounters in the West.

Theme 3: Social media & tourism choice

The role of platform aesthetics, “instagrammable” wellness, and digital representation in sustainable tourism decisions.

Open to collaboration

Co-authorship, invited talks, curriculum design, and policy-facing tourism research.
